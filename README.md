## 💡 Overview

<a href="#xferRecordsSerum">Serum</a> serves electronic music producers and sound designers across virtually every electronic genre — the synthesizer has become a standard tool in modern electronic music production for its combination of sound quality, visual workflow, and the sheer sonic range achievable through its synthesis engine.

The <a href="#xferRecordsSerum">electronic music production workflow</a> uses Serum as a primary sound source for bass, lead, pad, and pluck sounds across genres from dubstep and trap to house, techno, and pop production — the wavetable synthesis approach particularly suited to the evolving, modern timbres that contemporary electronic music favors over more traditional analog synthesis sounds. The <a href="#xferRecordsSerum">sound design specialist use case</a> uses Serum's visual wavetable editing and custom wavetable import for creating unique signature sounds — sound designers building distinctive sonic identities for artists or sample packs use Serum's flexibility to create sounds not achievable with more limited synthesis approaches.

The <a href="#xferRecordsSerum">DAW plugin integration</a> runs as VST, AU, and AAX format plugins compatible with virtually every professional digital audio workstation — Logic Pro, Ableton Live, FL Studio, Pro Tools, and others all support Serum as a standard instrument plugin in the production workflow. <a href="#xferRecordsSerum">Genuine sound design education value</a> — Serum's visual feedback makes it a popular tool for learning synthesis concepts, with the visual representation of wavetable manipulation helping users understand synthesis principles that remain abstract in non-visual synthesizers.</p>
